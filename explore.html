Wildlife | Nature | Forests

India is home to hundreds of wildlife sanctuaries, protecting diverse ecosystems and species like the Bengal tiger, one-horned rhinoceros, and Asiatic lion. Popular examples include Kaziranga National Park (Assam) for rhinos, Jim Corbett National Park (Uttarakhand) for tigers, the Sundarbans National Park (West Bengal) for its mangrove forests and tigers, and Gir National Park (Gujarat) for its unique Asiatic lions.
